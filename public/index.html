About Nüshu

Nüshu is the only writing system in the world designed and used exclusively by women, originating in the 19th century in Jiangyong County, Yongzhou City, in the southern part of Hunan Province, China. Local women secretly invented this script using branches dipped in soot from the bottom of cooking pots, passing it among themselves and not sharing it with men. It was used to create poems expressing their suffering.
